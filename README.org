* MiCA Provisions
- *Issuers' Duty:* Must publish whitepapers disclosing the "principal adverse impacts on the climate" caused by their consensus mechanisms, under:
  + Article 6(1) point (j): For general/utility tokens.
  + Article 19(1) point (h): For asset-referenced tokens (ARTs).
  + Article 51(1) point (g): For electronic money tokens (EMTs).
- *Service Providers' Obligations* (Article 66(5)): Mandates crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) to display these climate impact metrics prominently on their websites.

* AI Act Provisions
- *Obligated Subjects (Article 53(1)):* Energy obligations apply strictly to *providers* (natural or legal persons) who develop and place General Purpose AI (GPAI) models on the EU market.
- *The Reporting Mandate (Annex XI, Section 1, point 2(e)):* Providers must document the /"known or estimated energy consumption"/ of the model during its development/training.
- *The Downstream Exemption:* Downstream integrators (businesses building on top of LLMs) are *exempt* from energy reporting.
- *The Open-Source Exemption (Article 53(2)):* These transparency obligations are waived if the model is released under a free and open-source license.
- *The Systemic Risk Exception (Article 51):* The open-source exemption is completely voided if the model is classified as presenting a *systemic risk* (training compute exceeding 10^{25} FLOPs).
- *The Modification Trigger:* Downstream actors only inherit duties if they perform a /"significant modification"/ (e.g., fine-tuning with compute over 1/3 of original training).

* Commonalities
- *Strategy*: transparency.
- *Soft Governance:* Neither law directly caps carbon emissions or bans heavy computing; both reject technology bans in favor of disclosure.
- *Market-Driven:* Both assume public disclosures will steer investors and enterprise buyers toward cleaner tech.

* Gap 1: The Value Chain Blindspot
- *The Information Firewall:* Upstream providers report energy data to the *EU AI Office (Art 53(1)(a))*, but this data is *not public*.
- *The Annex XII Gap:* Information shared with downstream integrators is limited to technical specs; it *excludes environmental/energy metrics*.
- *Structural Blindness:* Downstream businesses face ESG pressure but legally lack the data to know the footprint of the foundational models they use.

* Gap 2: The Training vs. Inference Gap
- *The Training Fallacy:* The AI Act only tracks energy consumed during training (a one-off event).
- *The Inference Iceberg:* Ongoing, daily user queries (inference) represent the vast majority of AI's lifetime energy.
- *The Silent Footprint:* Millions of daily chats generate massive, unmonitored operational emissions.

* Gap 3: AI's Invisible "Thirst"
- *The Cooling Demands:* High-power chips require millions of liters of water daily to prevent overheating.
- *The Data:* A standard 20–50 query chat "drinks" roughly 500ml of fresh water.
- *The Regulatory Silence:* The AI Act mandates energy tracking but leaves water footprints entirely unregulated.

* The Future
- *Value Chain Transparency*
- *Inference Reporting*
- *Human Over Machine:* Technology must protect—not destroy—the resources that sustain human life.
- *Unified Policy:* Evolution — from separate to integrated environmental policy for tech law.
